The root issue (as your log confirms) is **audio context handling**: browsers block `AudioContext` creation unless triggered by a user gesture. So the sound must be **pre-initialized on the customer page**, then **triggered later via WebSocket or message event**.

However, your current system uses **Web Push**, **not WebSockets** — so the sound **cannot be triggered by a push event alone**, because **push events happen in the Service Worker**, and **you cannot play audio from a Service Worker**.

### ✅ Correct Technical Path:
To play sound **on the customer page** when a **Web Push arrives**, you must:
1. **Receive the push in the Service Worker** → show notification (already works).
2. **Also send a “message” from the Service Worker to the client page** (via `clients.matchAll` + `client.postMessage`).
3. **On the customer page**, listen for that message → then play the sound using a **pre-warmed AudioContext**.
